The Deceived Noble Soldier

Posted In React - By Donald John On Tuesday, August 9th, 2011 With 4 Comments

There is often a good amount of talk and even debate over the relationship between works and faith. You may be familiar with the verse “But will thou know, vain man, that faith without works is dead?”. Surely, one with faith in Jesus Christ need shew forth works, or “bear fruit” as it is often put. This article, however, outlines the marks of what I am calling the “Deceived Noble Soldier” – one full of works, but void of faith.

The deceived noble soldier is a person who may truly feel that it is of dire importance that society’s poor and lowly are taken care of. The deceived noble soldier will do community service, raise funds, feed the hungry, etc. with a zeal often unmatched. The deceived noble soldier believes what he or she is doing is just. Lastly, and most importantly, the deceived noble soldier denies the power of Jesus. Essencially, what I’m submitting to you is the image of a person wrapped up in care and compassion, but void of the faith which is more important than the sum of all their deeds.

Allow me to provide an example. Oprah Winfrey has donated millions upon millions of dollars to more causes than I can mention. I don’t need to speak to her works since they are plentiful and well known. She is a hard worker and she handles her business very well. Her compassion and concern for those in need is very evident. However, Oprah is unique among other talk show hosts and celebrities in that she openly talks about faith and religion, not in a distant and objective manner, but in a more personal way which almost hints at teaching or sharing certain ideas to the audience. This video will explain it all for you. I chose this clip because it has the most relevant clips in it (I don’t necessarily endorse the channel or the uploader’s overall message).

Quickly revisit the marks of this persona I am introducing. The compassion and noble works of Oprah are outstanding, however, the most important part, the faith behind those works, is missing. The bible teaches us that, without faith, it is impossible to please God. This faith should rest on Jesus the Christ, but – unfortunately as demonstrated in these clips – this is not the case with Oprah. Digest what you have just seen/read and you will have a great sense of what is meant by the phrase “deceived noble soldier”.

I am writing this to inform and warn God’s people against this form of service. This belief that God really only cares about what you do here on earth (caring and loving one another), and not whether you acknowledge Jesus as Lord is an abomination. Without acknowledging Jesus as the Christ who takes the wrath for us all, we will get caught up in legalism which is a judging of ourselves purely based on our works. At the base of this misguided idea is absurdity since even our best deeds are as dirty rags to God.

I call on you to spread this and begin to judge rightly and point out this abominable attitude wherever it manifests itself. Celebrities and music artists who promote an ungodly life in their profession, leading their audiences into snares – while at the same time opening foundations for the needy are other examples of this idea of deceived noble soldiers. They, as well as the rest of us, need to know or be reminded that our works are for naught – absolutely worthless – if they are not performed under loving obedience to Christ’s command.

Love
Donald John
