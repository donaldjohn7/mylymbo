Gay Lifestyles: Acceptance and Awareness

Posted In Bible Focus, Hear - By Donald John On Saturday, January 22nd, 2011 With 4 Comments


Article Link: http://www.wnd.com/?pageId=40339
The Above story was brought to my attention through a Christian apologist speaking on the homosexual agenda here in America. The article is about a father in Massachusetts who wanted to pull his child out of a section of her elementary school class because the school decided to introduce homosexual lifestyles suddenly in their curriculum. Specifically, a book “King and King” was being read which tells the story of a prince who marries another prince. The father, David Parker, addresses it and the matter is brought to court where the school’s choice in retaining all students while teaching on homosexual lifestyles is upheld. Parker is later arrested for refusing to leave a school meeting until his concern is addressed.
There is a difference between making the existence of homosexual relationships known to children and giving them the notion those relationships are ok. A very big difference. In the case above, this task has been handed over to the school system. Simply spoken, the children in the school system MUST be allowed to opt out of these teachings because they present a homosexual life style in a positive light (as if it is different but perfectly ok) – which is against Biblical truth.
I almost cower at the thought of what God is feeling on this issue. He has said, “It would be better to be thrown into the sea with a millstone hung around your neck than to cause one of these little ones to fall into sin.” (Luke 17:2) We have to take this very seriously.
On homosexuality:
Romans 1:26-27 reads, ”Because of this, God gave them over to shameful lusts. Even their women exchanged natural sexual relations for unnatural ones.  In the same way the men also abandoned natural relations with women and were inflamed with lust for one another. Men committed shameful acts with other men, and received in themselves the due penalty for their error.”
Go ahead and check out the whole chapter.
All scripture is from God. This passage is very clear in what it says. It is not the only of its kind. On this Christian journey, we must resist all lust and temptation, and above is another tenant for us to live by. If you are a Christian, and a practicing homosexual, now is the time to go to biblical truth. Do not delay. Do not seek those who will tell you what you want to here. You are not alone, all temptations are common to all men. You are not a sore thumb, however, you need to cling to God’s truth and hold to it with more force than your pride, temptation, or sexual identity. Again I say, seek God’s truth. Start with the passage above.
These are the times where we will see a direct clash with God’s truth and man’s doctrine. This story in Massachusetts sets America’s taste for diversity against God’s law. Again, there is a sharp difference between teaching the existence of gay relationships and teaching the acceptance of gay lifestyles as ok.
Donald John
