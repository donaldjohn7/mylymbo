Scripture: teaching a chicken latin
Jul 11th, 2011 @ 02:10 pm › Donald John
↓ Skip to comments


Do you truly believe all scripture is infallible? Are you prepared to stand by it whether it seems to contradict itself or not? Legions attack it, but the bible still remains the word of God. When things don’t seem clear, we are the problem, not the text.

Let me introduce you to what may seem to be a strange concept. Firstly, though, check this statement of faith that can be found in the back of many Baptist hymnals:

We believe the Holy Scriptures were written by men divinely inspired and is a perfect treasure of heavenly instruction, that has God for its author, that it reveals the principles by which God will judge us, therefore is, and shall remain to the end of the world, the true center of Christian union, and the supreme standard by which all human conduct, creeds and opinions should be tried.

Why these phrases???

  • Divinely inspired
  • Perfect treasure
  • True center of Christian union
  • Supreme standard

Reason #1 – the Bible is the word of God.
If you believe God is perfect, then His speaking will be sound and perfect as He is. He can’t be perfect but still make contradictions that mar His perfection.

Reason #2  – the Bible is the word of God.
God is so far above us its not even funny. Imagine trying to teach a chicken how to speak Latin. You could be the best Latin teacher in the universe, but the chicken won’t understand much… NOT because you are a bad teacher, but because the chicken is limited in its knowledge.

Reason #3 – the Bible is the word of God.
John writes, “In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God”(John 1:1). In Matthew, Jesus says, “Every kingdom divided against itself is brought to desolation; and every city or house divided against itself shall not stand.” (Matt 12:25b) Also, Jesus is the word made flesh. Knowing these things, we see that the Word of God is more than just letters on a page. It’s wrapped up into God Himself. God isn’t divided against Himself; neither is His Word.

I know that there are things I don’t understand in the bible. Sometimes, I wonder about life in general. But I must accept it all. For example, I accept that Jesus says “Come all ye heavy laden and I will give you rest” as well as “Take up your cross daily and follow me”. It’s up to me to trust God until there is understanding, not to question His word’s validity.

Love
Donald John
