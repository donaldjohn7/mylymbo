A woman in somalia – The Price of Pearls

Posted In Featured, feeling - By Myra Jacobs On Wednesday, August 18th, 2010 With 3 Comments

This song was originally sung by Sade.  It is entitled, “Pearls.”

There is a woman in Somalia.  Scraping for pearls on the roadside. There’s a force stronger than nature.  Keeps her will alive.  This is how she’s dying She’s dyin’ to survive. Don’t know what she’s made of. I would like to be that brave.  She cries to the heaven above. There is a stone in my heart. She lives a life she didn’t choose. And it hurts like brand new shoes. Hurts like brand new shoes. There is a woman in Somalia. The sun gives her no mercy. The same sky we lay under. Burns her to the bone. Long as afternoon shadows. It’s gonna take her to get home. Each grain carefully wrapped up. Pearls for her little girl. Hallelujah! Hallelujah! She cries to the heaven above. There is a stone in my heart. She lives a life she didn’t choose. And it hurts like brand-new shoes.

This song moved me the very first time I heard it.  It puts a face on poverty.  Can you see her?  She is a woman, like me.  Young, like me.  Brown like me.  But most importantly, HUMAN like me.  The world is off balance, and the gap between the rich and the poor is great.  If you are reading this, understand how blessed you are.  Electricity, literacy, having a roof over our head, clothes on our back, and food on our table is NOT the “norm” in this world.  Running water and a bed to sleep in are NOT the bare necessities for most people.  It’s time for the bubble to burst.  Pull the curtain.  Let our sheltered existences shatter!  There’s a whole other world out there.  We don’t have to ignore their faces as a means to self-medicate the overwhelming feelings of hopelessness that come in thinking that there is nothing we can do.  We are children of the light, and with God there is nothing we CAN’T do.  Find it, do it, start the REVOLUTION and in the mean time, don’t take life for granted.  It is too precious to waste.
