Battleground: from dead to alive

Posted In feeling, React, reacting - By Donald John On Wednesday, February 10th, 2010 With 2 Comments


Peace talks with satan never work, but war cries scare him to death.
I said that I will write for the Lord, and thats what I will do. Though sometimes it will feel much easier to do something else instead.

In Pittsburgh, the church we visited was a youthful one. People my age jumping, dancing, singing in the sanctuary. Even incorporating step dances into the praise…unique, and I was glad they did it. It was great, comfortable worship. But thats not why I’m writing this.

On the way (on the bus), I realized that the part of town we were in was very old. Since there were numerous churches on various corners, I was reminded of Baltimore and how it looks, with its corner-churches. A church here, a church there… Corner store here… Bar over there… Bars and churches often share the same block. Set up on corners, people come in and out. Both establishments center around the massing of people. Whether its a family coming into worship, or a group of guys going for a drink after work.

So these two entities battle together, toe to two. There is warfare in the city. The city is a war-grounds for two forces.

Warriors roam the streets and warriors comb the streets looking for someone to spread some good news to. Dead men and women walking serve as casualties in this Great War. Casualties due to drugs, crime, alcohol, many things…leaving the spirit dead and the body set on an animalistic roam to feed the habit that caused its death. In this war, there are many traitors and the battle lines are hard to spot. Enemy territory meets enemy territory, and there is no middle ground.

Now, any human using an unaided eye will look at this scene in these cities and assume since many dead walk the streets and crime riddles the sidewalks, leaving bullet holes in cars and windows, the battle…or war rather… can be given to Satan and his forces of evil.

BUT

If we could just stop and ask ourselves whose power outweighs who’s… who has the power to raise the dead? Where do the resurrected go to give up their lives??? It’s in the church! We give up our lives to God… freely.
Satan steals them, or does he… because those souls aren’t his and never will be. God resurrects what satan kills! It is proven every single day… Live life and pay attention, and you WILL see what I so eagerly speak about!

What is this power which causes my writing to speed… compromising the legibility of this note? Come and see! Come get your life back and give it up for something better. Eternal life.

Then we can live forever
Amen

…Peace talks with Satan never work out, but war cries scare him to death…

Donald John
