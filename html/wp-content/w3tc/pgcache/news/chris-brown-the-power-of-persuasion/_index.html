Chris Brown: The power of persuasion

Posted In News, reacting - By Donald John On Monday, April 26th, 2010 With 10 Comments


It’s no secret the legal trouble Chris Brown landed in a while back with Rihanna. However, I want to bring to your attention the change in image he has undergone and the reaction it has caused.

After the charges were brought and Chris Brown’s name was properly placed in the mouths of millions, he adopted a somber and apologetic image. He was, no doubt, in a very awkward situation, being a public model for many young and old alike. He even took his name of Nickelodeon’s Kids Choice Awards nomination roll because of the scandal.

He offered this quote:
“I am seeking the counseling of my pastor, my mother and other loved ones and I am committed, with God’s help, to emerging a better person.”

After this, he laid low for a while and time passed like it usually does. Both he and Rihanna have come out with new material since, but I was surprised to see so many defending him and his come-back after his image dropped so much.

My insides wants to shout out with: “Chris Brown supporters!! Why do you defend him so soon after he has been charged with the beating of a woman?”… but this post is about image and persuasion.
Brown has a new song out now called “Holla At Me”. The lyrics are very bold for someone who just was found to have beaten his girlfriend.

Here are some of the lyrics:
…knockin’ niqqaz over, call me bulldozer, one more drink for these *** and it’s over, cause i’m a strike that something like a cobra,
…Ah chu, bless me twice, be a rich nigga I be shitin’ on your life, magazine covers, magnum rubbers, I mean magnum, I don’t f*** with stragglers…
…blowin’ out there brains, car need a new roof, lookin’ like a superstar, when I roll thru and shawty I’m the truth, so mama what it do?…
…I’m the shit yeah I go hard, don’t stand in lines nigga I bogart fat boy celebrity cause I’m so large and don’t need no battery cause I’m in charge…

How can an audience listen to these lyrics from any figure who has had such a past? … A RECENT past?
Young people, as an audience, are being roped into things via persuasion. If the TV is playing his music again, everything must be ok. I urge you, reader, not to be persuaded into thinking that bad is good and good is bad! Please.
You doubt the power of persuasion? 6 years ago, you couldn’t have made any male within the hip hop culture to wear skin tight jeans. (Trust me, I was a part of that culture). Now, because popular society has suddenly started it, it seems every young person has a pair of skinny jeans.
Dare to disagree. Dare to agree.
Your’s truly
Donald John
